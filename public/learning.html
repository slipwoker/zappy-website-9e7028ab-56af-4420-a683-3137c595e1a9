What should I know about shipping live queen ants?

We ship queen ants with care using temperature-controlled packaging and expedited delivery to ensure they arrive healthy. Most species handle transit well when properly packaged. Timing and weather conditions are considered for every shipment, and we'll communicate any delays or concerns before your order ships.

Which setup is best for someone just starting out?

Beginner-friendly kits include a test tube setup or small formicarium designed for founding colonies. These provide the right humidity, visibility, and simplicity to help you learn ant care basics without overwhelming complexity. We recommend starting with hardy species likeLasius nigerorCamponotusfor your first colony.

How do I maintain proper humidity levels?

Most ant species thrive with humidity between 50-70% in the nest area, though requirements vary by species. Use a water reservoir or hydration chamber built into your formicarium, and monitor with a small hygrometer. Avoid over-saturating—ants need both humid nesting areas and dry foraging spaces.

How often can I safely observe my colony?

Brief daily observations (5-10 minutes) are generally safe and won't stress your ants. Avoid constant light exposure, tapping on the habitat, or moving the setup frequently. Founding queens especially need darkness and minimal disturbance during the first few weeks as they establish their brood.

How does colony expansion work as my ants grow?

As your colony grows from dozens to hundreds of workers, you'll connect additional modules or formicariums using tubing. Most setups are modular by design, allowing you to add outworlds, feeding areas, or nesting chambers as needed. We offer expansion kits compatible with your original habitat.

What should I do if my colony's behavior changes suddenly?

Changes in activity, aggression, or brood care can signal environmental issues—check temperature, humidity, and food supply first. Seasonal behavior shifts (like winter dormancy for temperate species) are normal. If problems persist, contact us with photos and details; we can help diagnose and resolve most colony health concerns.
